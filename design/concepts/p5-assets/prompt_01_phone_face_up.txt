A pixel art icon for a Chinese office life game: a single smartphone in face-up position lying on a wooden cubicle desk, screen black/off (idle state). To be used as a workstation prop layer that the player can tap.

Style: SFC/16-bit pixel art, visible pixel grid, no anti-aliasing on outlines, limited 16-color palette. NOT vector or smooth modern illustration. Visual identity goal: "first glance recognition, second glance indictment" — a familiar Chinese office aesthetic that quietly broadcasts dread. Mood: dark humor / 喜丧美学 (funeral-as-festival).

LAYOUT: Single isolated prop sprite, centered in canvas, surrounded by transparent / very thin desk sliver context only on bottom 1/8 of canvas. The phone occupies the central 60% of the canvas; rest is empty / minimal desk surface so engine can composite onto workstation BG.

CONTENT:
- Phone is a generic black flat smartphone (NO real-world brand, NO logo, NO Apple bite). Roughly 6:13 aspect ratio body.
- Top-down 3/4 angle view (slight tilt so we see the right edge thickness, ~2px).
- Visible features: large black screen (turned OFF — pure dark, very faint reflection), thin 1px white plastic bezel around screen, single physical home button at bottom (subtle dark gray circle), 2px speaker grill slot at top, micro front-camera dot 1px to the right of speaker.
- The phone case has a faint vinyl sticker visible in the bottom-right corner: a 4×3 px Mt. Fuji silhouette (continuity hint — protagonist's "8 年前去日本" memory). The sticker is muted, not glowing.
- Phone shadow falling 2-3px to the lower-right onto the desk.
- Background: only a tiny strip of wooden cubicle desk surface (使用 #7A5838 棕) at the bottom 1/8 of canvas — wood grain shown with 1-2px horizontal lines. Above the desk strip, neutral light off-white #E8E0CC.
